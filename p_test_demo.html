Two p-tests for a new batch

Compare the same batch against two candidate Gaussian distributions. Each p-value asks: would this batch mean be surprising under this candidate?

Interpretation

A small p-value means surprising under that candidate. A large p-value does not prove the candidate is true. Two p-tests are a compatibility check, not posterior probabilities. Increasing n makes the batch mean test more sensitive.
